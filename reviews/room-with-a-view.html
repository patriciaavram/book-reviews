A Room with a View

by E.M. Forster

Rating: ★★★☆☆

Summary

"But you do," he went on, not waiting for contradiction. "You love the boy body and soul, plainly, directly, as he loves you, and no other word expresses it ..." Lucy has her rigid, middle-class life mapped out for her, until she visits Florence with her uptight cousin Charlotte, and finds her neatly ordered existence thrown off balance. Her eyes are opened by the unconventional characters she meets at the Pension Bertolini: flamboyant romantic novelist Eleanor Lavish, the Cockney Signora, curious Mr Emerson and, most of all, his passionate son George. Lucy finds herself torn between the intensity of life in Italy and the repressed morals of Edwardian England, personified in her terminally dull fiancé Cecil Vyse. Will she ever learn to follow her own heart?

My Thoughts

This was a cute book, about a young woman finding herself through travel. She meets these very different personalities and begins to expand her horizons beyond the proper way of living she was taught, leading her to fall in love with a man who everyone thinks is not a suitable and upright young man to be with. Naturally, we see her grow and evolve into a more confident woman, more vocal about what she wants or doesn't want. I just wish she could've come to these conclusions on her own, since most of the time people were pushing her to do things rather than her getting those ideas and pushing herself.

I really wanted to like this book more than I did, I had to follow along with summaries of each chapter just because I felt like I was missing something during my reading. I understand that the first part was about her travels, but I didn't understand many of the location references in Florence, and it caused me to be confused at times. Part 2 was much easier to understand, which was nice, and really dove deeper into her evolution as a person than in part 1. I assume part 1's purpose was mostly to introduce the characters and set up the actual plot for part 2, so it makes sense in that way. Overall, it wasn't a difficult book to read, and it might just be my fault that I wasn't able to follow along with part 1 very well, but I liked the story nonetheless.
