The Midnight Library

by Matt Haig

Rating: ★★⯪☆☆

Summary

Between life and death there is a library. When Nora Seed finds herself in the Midnight Library, she has a chance to make things right. Up until now, her life has been full of misery and regret. She feels she has let everyone down, including herself. But things are about to change. The books in the Midnight Library enable Nora to live as if she had done things differently. With the help of an old friend, she can now undo every one of her regrets as she tries to work out her perfect life. But things aren't always what she imagined they'd be, and soon her choices place the library and herself in extreme danger. Before time runs out, she must answer the ultimate question: what is the best way to live?

My Thoughts

The premise of this novel was interesting but in execution it just showed a very simplistic and flat view of depression and suicide (e.g. suicidal thoughts can be solved by simply looking back on your regrets and the lives that you could've led if you hadnt made a certain choice.) The author kept implying that the only way that a woman can truly be happy is by having the marriage and child combination, which might be the case for some women but it felt forced in the writing. Also I just wanted to mention she's 30… the author implies that her life is over because she isn't married, and just lost her job and her cat... Life doesn't end at grief, and 30 years is only a third of your life.

The writing felt pretty repetitive, I understand the necessary sequence of events that keep adding onto her doubt about wanting to end her life, but I feel as it could've been half the length and the message would've been unaffected. I thought that there should've been less events, but they should've been more lengthy. She should've been exploring each life more in depth to gauge how she felt about it, and I would've liked it more. At times, it felt like scrolling motivational quotes on Tumblr or Pinterest.

Overall, an alright book, dare I say a good book for reading to relax on an empty brain.
