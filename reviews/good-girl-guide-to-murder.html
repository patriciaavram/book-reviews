A Good Girl's Guide to Murder

by Holly Jackson

Rating: ★★★★☆

Summary

The case is closed. Five years ago, schoolgirl Andie Bell was murdered by Sal Singh. The police know he did it. Everyone in town knows he did it. But having grown up in the same small town that was consumed by the crime, Pippa Fitz-Amobi isn't so sure. When she chooses the case as the topic for her final project, she starts to uncover secrets that someone in town desperately wants to stay hidden. And if the real killer is still out there, how far will they go to keep Pip from the truth…?

My Thoughts

Very catchy murder mystery, easily consumed in a weekend, with characters that have a lot of personality, and a plot full of twists and turns. I was actually very excited to read this novel, and it did not disappoint. It was exactly the quick and crunchy read that I was expecting. I'm glad that this book was heavily centered around actually solving the murder, with a slight romantic side, and not focused on the romance itself. I really liked this novel, I tried to predict who the killer was at multiple points in the book as more information was revealed, but I really didn't expect there to be two of them. I liked the authors' creativity in creating the characters, because they were all layered characters with different reasons for disliking or wanting Andie Bell to be hurt. Overall, each character was interesting to read about, and added value to the plot such that the story felt richer even when Pip was investigating a lead and there was a lull in the action.

So many plot twists that I wasn't expecting. I had a hunch that Mr. Ward was somehow involved in her death because of how the author deescribed their altercations in the classroom, so I was slightly confused that Pip took Mr.Ward off the suspects list so soon, but it might have been because she assumed there was no way he was guilty since she's known him since childhood. I also did not expect Becca to be the one actually responsible for Andie's death, nor was I expecting her to be responsible for kidnapping Pip's dog. There are still some loose ends in terms of the persons of interest list that Pip drew up. I feel like Max and Howie's stories are still not over though... there was a sense of unfinishedness when Pippa presented to the press. I will definitely be continuing the series because Holly Jackson's plot and writing style was right up my alley.
