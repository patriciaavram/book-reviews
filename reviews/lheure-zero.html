L'Heure Zéro

by Agatha Christie

Rating: ★★★★☆

Summary

L'Heure zéro (Towards Zero) est un roman policier d'Agatha Christie publié en revue en mai 1944 puis en volume en juin 1944 aux États-Unis. En France, il est publié en 1947. C'est le cinquième et dernier roman mettant en scène le superintendant Battle. Le récit évoque le champion de tennis Nevile Strange qui rassemble pour des vacances, sous le prétexte d'en faire des amies, son ex-femme Audrey Strange, et sa nouvelle compagne Kay Mortimer-Strange. L'idée n'est pas du goût de lady Tressillian, tante de Nevile et propriétaire de la villa où tout ce beau monde se retrouve. Nevile a gardé un petit faible pour son ex-femme, laquelle inspire un amour sans espoir à son cousin Thomas. Autour de ce quatuor, on retrouve Edward Latimer, un ami de Kay qui aimerait bien être plus qu'un ami, et M. Treves, vieil avocat à la retraite en pleine possession de ses capacités en matière de criminologie… Lorsque lady Tressillian est retrouvée dans son propre lit, le crâne défoncé par un coup de club de golf bien appliqué, que faut-il penser ?

My Thoughts

Classic whodunit, with interesting characters, rivalries and behavioural patterns. I did feel that Neville was awfully annoying throughout the whole book, which should've made a light go off in my head about his involvement in the murder, but I unfortunately overlooked it. Thought the pacing was a bit slow at first, kept wondering what's going to happen, who's going to die, and was slightly confused and surprised by the death of M. Treves. Still unsure what the correlation of this particular person's death was with the story, I feel like it could've been skipped and the story wouldn't have been hurt. I admit it did add some creepiness aspect to the mood, and made all the characters more nervous. I really liked how the author introduced different characters, who at first had no connection to the story, eventually ended up getting involved, like Battle's daughter's story, and the man who had tried to end his life by jumping from the same cliff as Audrey. The plot twist was quite unexpected, but of course, the quality of the story was extremely good, given who the writer is. Agatha has a way of making us believe every new piece of evidence and theory thought of by the police officers; I have no idea how she does it every time. Overall a good story to devour in one sitting.
