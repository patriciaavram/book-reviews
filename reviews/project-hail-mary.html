Project Hail Mary

by Andy Weir

Rating: ★★★★⯪

Summary

Ryland Grace is the sole survivor on a desperate, last-chance mission—and if he fails, humanity and the earth itself will perish. Except that right now, he doesn’t know that. He can’t even remember his own name, let alone the nature of his assignment or how to complete it. All he knows is that he’s been asleep for a very, very long time. And he’s just been awakened to find himself millions of miles from home, with nothing but two corpses for company. His crewmates dead, his memories fuzzily returning, Ryland realizes that an impossible task now confronts him. Hurtling through space on this tiny ship, it’s up to him to puzzle out an impossible scientific mystery—and conquer an extinction-level threat to our species. And with the clock ticking down and the nearest human being light-years away, he’s got to do it all alone. Or does he?

My Thoughts

First of all, Rocky is the cutest alien that has ever been written in history. I really liked this entire story and thoughts that a lot of aspects of his travels in space were really original. It felt as every discovery in space was actually pretty complex and probably took a lot of imagination to build around them. For instance, finding Xenonite and investigating how it works and its interactions with Taumoeba, as well as the process of figuring out the ecosystem surrounding Astrophage. Coming up with an alien, coming up with its biology and how it relates to the environment on its home planet was incredibly clever and I'm sure it took an insane amount of research. Describing the inner workings of a spaceship, complete with technology that doesn't exist yet like the coma-induced machine that he used, must've been a feat within itself.

The only thing that annoyed me at times is that I feel like the book could've been shortened 50 pages. There were a lot of non necessary passages where Ryland was dumping scientific facts that werent relevant to the story, or even to the ongoing science experiments. This book was definitely meant to be introspective and maybe some people really do think that much and go on tangents when they do science experiments, I don't really know how the brain works. I also thought Ryland was a goofy character but some of his lingo took me a while to get used to, all the "What the heck"s and the "Gosh darn it"s were corny at the beginning.

Overall, this was a very good story, I'm definitely excited to see the movie and see how they adapted the introspectiveness of it to work with the visuals. The ending was very sweet but slightly predictable, I had a hunch that he would turn back around and join Rocky.
