1984

by George Orwell

Rating: ★★★★⯪

Summary

A masterpiece of rebellion and imprisonment where war is peace freedom is slavery and Big Brother is watching. Thought Police, Big Brother, Orwellian - these words have entered our vocabulary because of George Orwell's classic dystopian novel 1984. The story of one man's Nightmare Odyssey as he pursues a forbidden love affair through a world ruled by warring states and a power structure that controls not only information but also individual thought and memory 1984 is a prophetic haunting tale More relevant than ever before 1984 exposes the worst crimes imaginable the destruction of truth freedom and individuality.

1984 - Goodreads

My Thoughts

I was skeptical about Julia at first, I thought she might've been a member of the thought police, it just seemed too good to be true that someone would be as rebellious as Winston. I thought the book evolved a bit slow in the beginning, which makes sense as it was setting up a routine to show the repetitiveness of Winston's life. It helped that I knew a bit of the history of the political landscape at that time, as it was similar to stories described by my family who were near the USSR during the Cold War. Did not enjoy the way Winston thinks about women, just full of violent and nasty thoughts.

This book was amazing overall, but Part 3 had some of the most exquisite monologues I've ever read, each one more beautifully written than the other. I don't normally read books of this genre but this one rightfully earns its title as a classic, especially given the current political landscapes. The story was easy to follow and captivating. However, it did stall for me during some parts where Winston was reading the Brotherhood's doctrine. Just felt tedious to get through at times, but in hindsight, it was necessary to the storyline. Winston and Julia's bonding was interesting to see but to be honest, I most enjoyed witnessing O'Brien's implication in the plot, truly a masterpiece of a character. The ending was unexected, I didn't think that they would let him live after all he did, and he did explicitly say that no one ever comes back from room 101.
