Sunrise on the Reaping

by Suzanne Collins

Rating: ★★★★⯪

Summary

As the day dawns on the fiftieth annual Hunger Games, fear grips the districts of Panem. This year, in honor of the Quarter Quell, twice as many tributes will be taken from their homes. Back in District 12, Haymitch Abernathy is trying not to think too hard about his chances. All he cares about is making it through the day and being with the girl he loves. When Haymitch's name is called, he can feel all his dreams break. He's torn from his family and his love, shuttled to the Capitol with the three other District 12 tributes: a young friend who's nearly a sister to him, a compulsive oddsmaker, and the most stuck-up girl in town. As the Games begin, Haymitch understands he's been set up to fail. But there's something in him that wants to fight... and have that fight reverberate far beyond the deadly arena.

My Thoughts

Absolutely devastating book, Suzanne you have done it again! This series is the earliest series I started reading, and it will always be close to my heart. I didn't fully enjoy the previous book, The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes, but this one was an absolute masterpiece. Suzanne managed to capture the setting of District 12 perfectly, it almost felt like reading about Katniss' story again.

The characters were beautifully written, even the ones that I disliked, such as Snow, Plutarch or Drusilla were very complex and developed. Speaking of Drusilla, even she was clearly manipulated by the Capitol. Even the tributes that were unlikeable such as Silka or Panache were their own people with families and stories, and unfortunately were also playthings for the Capitol's amusement. Lou-lou, Ampert, Maysilee, all suffered so much. Lou-lou's story broke my heart and this just shows the lengths that the Capitol will go to to disguise the truth, really makes you think about media manipulation. Louella's family deserved to know the truth.

Unfortunately all of them suffered horrendous fates, that was to be expected though. They also really couldn't let Beetee live peacefully after winning could they, they had to reduce him to the minimum of a human being, and put his son through everything he had to go through? Reminder that you're never free after the Games, that the Capitol keeps you alive, but obviously the long term effects of the trauma ruins your life.

One thing I will mention though, is that the added plot of the Coveys (nomadic musical group forced to settle in District 12), wasn't present in the original books in sort of threw me off. Feels like the author added this aspect to make two ends of the story meet. Also wasn't sure if I found the fact that Haymitch kept calling his girlfriend by her full name cute or a bit odd. I guess the movie will confirm or deny this for me.

When the Games ended around 80% through the book, I was confused because I couldn't imagine anything else that could go wrong. And then BAM they kill his entire family?? To be fair, I saw something coming when Haymitch mocked Snow with the milk. There was no way that Snow was going to let him get away with that, but that was especially horrendous to read about. The gruelling reality of the Capitol delivering the bodies to their families the way they did upset me a lot, they couldn't even let them rest in peace. Suzanne had never described the aftermath of the games, and I had to put the book down after reading about the families having to identify the bodies of their children in the caskets.

The movie adaptation will absolutely break me for sure.
