White Oleander

by Janet Fitch

Rating: ★★★★⯪

Summary

Everywhere hailed as a novel of rare beauty and power, White Oleander tells the unforgettable story of Ingrid, a brilliant poet imprisoned for murder, and her daughter, Astrid, whose odyssey through a series of Los Angeles foster homes--each its own universe, with its own laws, its own dangers, its own hard lessons to be learned--becomes a redeeming and surprising journey of self-discovery.

White Oleander - Goodreads

My Thoughts

Don't think I've ever read a book quite like this one. An unfair and extremely sad story, which unfortunately I imagine represents reality for a lot of foster children; a story about how the lessons from people you meet in life, no matter how cruel they are, shape you for life. I was glad to see that no matter how much garbage life threw at Astrid, she didn't waver in her core morals and values. Of course, depending on the foster family, she had to mold into different people, but at her most basic state, even when she was prioritizing her own survival, she helped others as well as helping herself. In simpler words, this is a story of a mother that should never have had a child, showcasing the repercussions of this decision on a child who wants nothing more but to be loved and cared for, and for someone to know her soul, like Claire did.

Her uncooperative and selfish mother made a great villain though, I often found myself getting frustrated with her, pretending that everything was fine when she had put herself first instead of her daughter, gaslighting Astrid about how she should behave in her foster homes, manipulating her when she had finally found a good home and a good rythm with her foster parent(s). Trying to sabotage everything in Astrid's life out of spite simply because of her own miserable situation.

At times, the amount of trauma Astrid experienced in her foster homes felt overwhelming, Fitch has a great way of writing her prose that really makes you feel awful when things go badly in her characters' lives. Really enjoyed the friendship between Astrid, Niki and Yvonne especially, an unlikely but beautiful relationship that emerged from suffering. Wish I could've seen more of it. Wonderful book, definitely rereadable and will definitely watch the movie. I am ready to have my heart shattered into a million pieces and put together again.
